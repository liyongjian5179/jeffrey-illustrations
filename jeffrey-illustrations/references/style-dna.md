# Style DNA

Jeffrey Warm Tech Illustration is a warm, personal technical illustration style.

## Must have

- warm hand-drawn look with subtle crayon / colored pencil texture on characters, props and outlines
- clean thick outlines
- high whitespace
- cream background
- Jeffrey Blue and Warm Yellow brand colors
- simple visual metaphor
- short Chinese handwritten notes only when needed

## Palette

When no reference image is attached, put these exact color anchors into the prompt:

- Hoodie main blue: `#5379E8`
- Headphone / outline accent blue: `#2F56C5`
- Warm yellow accent: `#FFD84A`
- Cream background: `#FFF9EE`
- Denim jeans: `#88A8F5`
- Hair / line dark: `#2B2F36`

Avoid purple-blue, cyan, orange, brown hair, gray backgrounds and random accent colors.

## Texture

Texture should be selective:

- Characters, clothing, hair, Byte, props and outlines can show subtle crayon / colored pencil grain.
- Background should stay mostly flat cream, clean and quiet.
- Labels, cards and UI-like panels should remain readable, with only light hand-drawn outline texture.
- Shadows should be sparse and soft.

Avoid heavy full-image crayon grain, dirty paper texture, smudged labels and noisy text areas.

## Must not have

- dark hacker room
- cyberpunk neon
- realistic rendering
- color drift away from `#5379E8`, `#2F56C5`, `#FFD84A`, `#FFF9EE`, `#88A8F5`, `#2B2F36`
- heavy full-image crayon grain or dirty paper background
- complicated UI
- PPT-style chart overload
- large text blocks
- decorative props that distract from the metaphor
- random outfit changes

## Mood

Warm, calm, curious, technical, builder-like, lightly humorous.

## Composition Modes

### Warm Article Mode

Use for WeChat articles, blogs and narrative article body illustrations.

- Allows a few creator-context props such as notebook, coffee mug, plant, pencil cup or desk tools.
- Props must support the warm creator scene.
- Props must not become the main subject or crowd the metaphor.

### Clean Skill Mode

Use for README, GitHub docs, Skill examples, Notion / Obsidian notes and method diagrams.

- Keep only objects required by the metaphor.
- Avoid decorative desk props.
- Prefer more whitespace and fewer background elements.
